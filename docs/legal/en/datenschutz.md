## Purpose

MC Test is a Streamlit app for selecting, creating, practicing and evaluating multiple-choice question sets. The app uses pseudonyms instead of real names. Please do not enter real names, student IDs, email addresses or other personal data in pseudonyms, free-text fields or uploaded question sets.

## Data Processed by the App

The app stores pseudonyms, technical user identifiers derived from them, test sessions, selected question sets, mode, tempo, timestamps, answers, scores, correctness, optional confidence values, bookmarks, feedback reports, result summaries, optional recovery secrets as salt and hash only, language and tempo preferences for reserved pseudonyms, heartbeats for active sessions, and admin/security logs where relevant.

Uploaded or pasted question sets are stored temporarily with their content, upload time, original file name and uploader pseudonym or hash.

## Hosting

The app is hosted on Streamlit Community Cloud at `mc-test.streamlit.app`. The platform provider may process technically necessary access data such as IP address, time of access, browser/device information and requested URL to deliver, secure and operate the app.

## Legal Basis and Retention

Processing is based on Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR: the legitimate interest in operating a learning and practice application securely and reliably. The recovery secret is optional.

Unreserved pseudonyms are usually released after the configured release period, currently 24 hours. Related sessions, answers, bookmarks and feedback are deleted; result summaries may remain for leaderboards and evaluations. Temporary user question sets are usually cleaned up after 24 hours, or up to 14 days for reserved pseudonyms.

## External Services

The app itself does not call external LLM APIs and does not use its own analytics or advertising tools. The app does not set its own tracking, analytics or advertising cookies. For technical operation, Streamlit Community Cloud may use strictly necessary cookies, local browser storage or comparable technologies, for example to manage sessions and deliver the app.

## Your Rights

Under the GDPR, you may have rights to access, rectification, erasure, restriction, data portability and objection. You may also lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority. For requests, use the email address above and include your pseudonym where possible.
